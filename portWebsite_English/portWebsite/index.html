What is
diffusion-limited aggregation?

Random Walk
Image from https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion-limited_aggregation

Diffusion-limited aggregation (DLA) is the process where particles undergo a random walk due to Brownian motion cluster together to form aggregates of such particles. This model was proposed by T.A. Witten and L.M. Sander in 1981 and was originally used to explain the coalescence of particles such as metal powders suspended in solution and the atmosphere. After DLA was proposed,it had received attention from scholars in various fields because similar coalescence are prevalent in nature, such as dielectric breakdown, electrodeposition at liquid interfaces, etc.
