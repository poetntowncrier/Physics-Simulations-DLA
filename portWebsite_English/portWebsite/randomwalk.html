Random Walk

A random walk is a random process that describes a path that consists of a succession of random steps.
Every random walk corresponds to a diffusion law.

Random Walk
Image from https://www.wikiwand.com/simple/Random_walk


First proposed in 1905 by Karl Pearson, a random walk is a mathematical statistical model consisting of a sequence of trajectories, each of which is random. Random walks have many applications in various fields, including ecology, psychology, computer science, physics, chemistry, biology, and economics. Additionally, they can be used to simulate the paths of molecules as they travel through liquids or gases, the search paths of foraging animals, and fluctuating stock prices.
