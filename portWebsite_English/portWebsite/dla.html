Diffusion Limited Aggregation

Diffusion-limited aggregation (DLA) is the process where particles undergo a random walk due to Brownian motion cluster together to form aggregates of such particles.


This model was proposed by T.A. Witten and L.M. Sander in 1981 and was originally used to explain the coalescence of particles such as metal powders suspended in solution and the atmosphere. After DLA was proposed,it had received attention from scholars in various fields because similar coalescence are prevalent in nature, such as dielectric breakdown, electrodeposition at liquid interfaces, etc.

(a) DLA in Bacterial Growth
Fujikawa, H., & Matsushita, M. (1989). Fractal Growth ofBacillus subtilison Agar Plates. Journal of the Physical Society of Japan, 58(11), 3875-3878.

(b) DLA in Electrodeposition of zinc.
Mhíocháin, T. R. N., & Coey, J. M. D. (2004). Chirality of electrodeposits grown in a magnetic field. Physical Review E, 69(6).

DLA starts from the centre of the lattice grid. Particles difuse by random walking. When a particle hits an existing point, it sticks to the point. As the numbers of stuck particles get larger, we will get a DLA pattern. The first slider controls the moving radius of particles. The second slider controls the rate of random walk.
