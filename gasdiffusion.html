Gas Diffusion

Diffusion is a physical process where the molecules of liquid or gas move randomly. The molecules moves from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration.

Gas Diffusion
Image from https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diffusion

In 1848, Scottish chemist Thomas Graham proposed Graham's Laws (also known as Graham's Laws of Diffusion and Effusion). This formulat is stated as:
\[{Rate\;of\;A \over Rate\;of\;B} = {\sqrt{M_{B}} \over \sqrt{M_{A}}}\] Rate of A is the rate of diffusion(effusion) for the first gas. Rate of B is the rate of diffusion(effusion) for the second gas. MA is the molar mass of gas A MB is the molar mass of gas 2. Graham's law states that the rate of diffusion(effusion) of a gas is inversely proportional to the square root of its molecular weight. In other words, heavier gas has slower rate of diffusion(effusion).
